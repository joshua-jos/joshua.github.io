APPLICATION OF GEOSPATIAL TECHNOLOGY FOR ENVIRONMENTAL ASSESSMENT OF THAMIRABARANI DELTA REGION IN THOOTHUKUDI DISTRICT

        Global warming and human activities are rapidly contributing to environmental degradation. Effective monitoring of the environment and understanding requires valuable data that can be extracted through application of geospatial technologies such as remote sensing and GIS that can be used effectively for environmental assessment and planning. In this study Remote Sensing and GIS has been utilised to derive the landuse and land cover, LST, NDVI, water level and rainfall maps for a period of 15 years, in parts of Thamirabarani delta region, Tuticorin district, Tamil Nadu, India using multi-temporal remote sensing data. LULC maps for 2004, 2009, 2014 and 2019 were prepared based on NRSC level 2 classification. LST and NDVI maps were prepared from multi-temporal LANDSAT data. Water level and rainfall maps for pre monsoon and post monsoon were prepared from the data collected from PWD. Change detection analysis was done for LULC, LST, NDVI, Rainfall and Water level maps. It shows the larger area in plantations, scrub lands, fallows and barren lands are converted into built ups and it increases gradually in the period of 15 years. There is also a significant increase in crop land and plantation due to increased agricultural activities. LST has increased in built-up areas and decreased in vegetation covered areas. In NDVI, there is a gradual increase in the density of vegetation in crop lands due to the increase in agricultural activities. Whereas, the urban area has undergone gradual decrease in the vegetal cover. Correlation studies were carried out between NDVI and LST. The correlative analysis between NDVI and LST of 2004 to 2019, demonstrate that there is a negative correlation in between them. That is in urban areas the NDVI values decrease with increase of LST, but at the same time in dense vegetation area NDVI values increase with decrease of Land Surface Temperature.

        Saline water intrusion study was done using GALDIT model. The results show that coastal areas, namely, Thoothukudi, Kayalpatnam and Thiruchendur are the moderately vulnerable zones. In the areas like Srivaikuntam, Mukkani, Angamangalam have been noticed as low vulnerability condition. The saline water intrusion has reduced during 2019 compared with 2009 because of construction of check dams in Mukkani and Latchumipuram that, has increased the sediment concentration. Based on the Model results recommendations are suggested for the sustainable environmental management and development.

ASSESSMENT OF LAND DEGRADATION IN DHARMAPURI DISTRICT USING REMOTE SENSING AND GEOSPATIAL TECHNIQUES

         Land degradation, a current environmental threat of global proportions, is a complex issue which needs an inter- disciplinary approach in order to tackle a given territory's sensitivity to the process. This study aims to spatially analyse the land degradation sensitivity in Dharmapuri taluk, a region currently known for this type of environmental deterioration. The analysis is based on mapping and assessing land susceptibility to degradation by means of a multifactorial approach using GIS techniques, such as the well-established MEDALUS (Mediterranean Desertification and Land Use) method. Widely used in the Mediterranean region and other areas worldwide due to advantageous features such as simplicity, flexibility and rapid implementation, the method was used in this study for the analysis of the main indicators identified to be driving forces of land degradation, i.e. Climate Quality Index, Soil Quality Index, Vegetation Quality Index and Management Quality Index. Each parameter is quantified according to the de- fined method by giving them a sensitivity coefficient between 1.0 and 2.0. ArcGIS 10.0 has been applied to analyze and prepare layers of quality maps. Subsequently, the geometric mean for all four quality indicators was used to generate the map of environmental sensitivity to degradation. The modified MEDALUS method used nine factors from 1994 to 2019 quantify the sensitivity of land to degradation. The results obtained show that in 1994, 186sq.km of the study area is classified as highly critical, 555sq.km classified as very critical and 212sq.km is moderately critical and 35sq.km of the surface as very fragile and about 3sq.km as fragile to degradation processes and in 2019, 566sq.km classified as very critical and 212sq.km is moderately critical and 15sq.km of the surface as very fragile to degradation processes. The results have also shown that MEDALUS model is a functional tool for simulations which support sustainable land management in the areas prone to degradation.
